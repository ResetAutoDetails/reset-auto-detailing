Why Tint

What Window Tint Actually Does

Heat Rejection

Tinted windows block solar heat, keeping your interior significantly cooler in summer. Ceramic film blocks up to 60%+ of solar heat.

Privacy & Security

Darkened windows make it harder to see into your car — protecting valuables and giving you more privacy while driving.

Glare Reduction

Reduce eye strain from direct sunlight and oncoming headlights — improving comfort and safety on the road.

UV Blocking

Quality film blocks up to 99% of UV rays — protecting your skin during long drives and preventing your interior from fading.

Interior Protection

UV and heat cause dashboards, seats, and upholstery to crack and fade over time. Tint significantly slows that damage.

Cleaner Look

Tinted windows give any car a more finished, premium look. Properly installed film is clean and bubble-free.

Legal Limits

Illinois & Indiana Tint Laws

VLT (Visible Light Transmission) refers to the percentage of light that passes through the film. Lower % = darker tint. We only install legal shades — always confirm with us before selecting.

Illinois (IL)

Windshield Non-reflective tint above AS-1 line only
Front Side Windows 35% VLT minimum
Rear Side Windows Any darkness
Rear Window Any darkness

Indiana (IN)

Windshield Non-reflective above AS-1 line only
Front Side Windows 30% VLT minimum
Rear Side Windows 30% VLT minimum
Rear Window 30% VLT minimum

Laws may change. Always verify current regulations with the IL or IN Secretary of State. We advise on legal options when you book.
