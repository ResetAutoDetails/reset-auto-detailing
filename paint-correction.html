What It Does

What Paint Correction Actually Fixes

Paint correction uses a machine polisher and compound or polish to remove a microscopic layer of clear coat — taking the defects with it. The result is a smooth, reflective surface that looks like the paint was just applied.

✗

Swirl Marks

Caused by improper washing or automatic car washes. Most visible in direct sunlight.

✗

Light Scratches

Surface-level scratches in the clear coat from fingernails, keys, brushes, or debris.

✗

Water Spots

Mineral deposits left from hard water or rain that etch into the clear coat over time.

✗

Oxidation & Dullness

UV exposure breaks down the clear coat over time, leaving paint looking faded or chalky.

Paint Correction Before & After

What paint correction can't fix:

Deep scratches that penetrate through the clear coat into the base coat or primer require repainting — not polishing. We'll tell you upfront if a scratch is too deep to correct.

How It Works

The Paint Correction Process

1

Decontamination Wash

We start with a thorough wash and clay bar treatment to remove all surface contamination before polishing begins.

2

Paint Inspection

We inspect the paint under focused lighting to identify defect levels and confirm which correction level is needed.

3

Machine Polishing

We work panel by panel with a dual-action or rotary polisher, using the appropriate compound and polish combination for your paint.

4

Protection Applied

Once correction is complete, we apply a wax, sealant, or ceramic coating to protect the freshly corrected surface.
